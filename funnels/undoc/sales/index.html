I assumed I needed to try harder. Eat better. Sleep better. Be calmer. Be less stressed.

But my life had not suddenly become impossible. My body and brain had become less predictable—and I had no language for it.

Once I started learning what perimenopause can actually look like, I felt less frightened. Not because everything changed overnight, but because I finally had somewhere practical to start.
